title: Hover Focus
description: The single hover-focus system every NQChart follows — one contract, no per-chart guesswork

Every NQChart uses the **same** hover behavior. There is one contract, applied
uniformly; individual charts do not invent their own hover animation.

## The contract

On hover:

- The **focused mark group** stays at **full opacity**.
- **Every other mark dims to `0.2` opacity** (`HOVER_DIM_OPACITY`).
- **Nothing else changes** — no scale, no shadow "pop", no size jump. The focus
  mechanism is *opacity only*.

That's it. Whether it's a bar, a pie slice, or a radar polygon, the hovered thing
stays bright and the rest fade back. The single source of truth is
[`emphasis-presets.ts`](https://github.com/nqlib/nqchart/blob/main/src/registry/echarts-core/emphasis-presets.ts).

## Two mappings, one contract

The contract is implemented two ways, chosen by **geometry** — never by taste:

| Mapping | Charts | ECharts config |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Index focus** | bar, line, area, composed, waterfall | `focus: "self"`, `blurScope: "coordinateSystem"` (axis tooltip) |
| **Item focus** | pie, funnel, radar, heatmap, treemap, calendar, scatter | `focus: "self"`, `blurScope: "series"` (item tooltip) |
| **Series focus** | radial bar, rose | one series per ring/petal + `focus: "series"` |

- **Index focus** — charts share a category axis. Hovering a category keeps that
  whole column bright and dims the rest. Driven by the axis tooltip.
- **Item focus** — single-series charts. Hovering one segment keeps it bright and
  dims its siblings. Driven by the item tooltip. Scatter compiles every `<Scatter />`
  into **one** ECharts series and copies `itemFocus()` onto **each data row** (Symbol
  ignores series-level emphasis when rows are object-shaped).
- **Series focus** — polar bars. Each ring (or petal) is its **own series**, so
  `focus: "series"` highlights the hovered ring and dims the others. This is the
  official ECharts polar-bar pattern; doing it per data-item (`focus: "self"`)
  instead thrashes the dim across the gaps between rings (flicker), so polar bars
  must be modeled as one-series-per-ring. State changes are instant
  (`stateAnimation.duration: 0`) to avoid interpolating gradient fills.

All three produce the *identical visual result*: one thing bright, everything else
at `0.2`.

## Why the blur shapes differ (they don't change the look)

Each chart dims through whichever style channels it actually draws —
`itemStyle` for bars and tiles, plus `lineStyle` for lines and radar polygons, plus
`areaStyle` for filled areas. This is **not** a stylistic choice: ECharts corrupts
bar columns if you blur their `lineStyle`, and throws if you blur
`areaStyle` on a lines-only radar. The opacity value (`0.2`) and the focus mode are
constant everywhere — only the channel that carries the opacity varies.

## The one sanctioned exception: `*-glow` variants

The explicit glow variants — `area-glow`, `scatter-glow`, `radar-glow` — add their
signature shadow to the focused mark on top of the opacity focus. This is a
**variant style**, opted into by name, not a hover animation. Default charts pass
no shadow and therefore never pop.

## Exceptions: tooltip-only charts

Two cases are **tooltip-only** — they show the tooltip on hover but do not dim:

- **Gauge / target** (single-row radial) — a single arc has no siblings to dim.
- **Sparkline** — decorative; emphasis disabled (its gradient area fill also can't be
  interpolated in a state transition).

Radial bar / rose used to be tooltip-only for two reasons — gradient
`itemStyle.color` can't be interpolated in an emphasis tween (throws
`Cannot read properties of undefined (reading 'length')`), and per-item focus
flickered across the ring gaps. **Series focus solves both**: modeling each ring as
its own series means `focus: "series"` never hit-tests per item (no gap flicker), and
`stateAnimation.duration: 0` skips the tween entirely (no gradient interpolation). So
polar bars now follow the full dim-to-`0.2` contract — see "Series focus" above.

## Flicker control

HTML tooltips render over the canvas. To stop the pointer from ever landing on the
tooltip DOM (which would bounce hover on/off), the tooltip is `pointer-events: none`,
offset from the cursor, and uses `transitionDuration: 0`. See
[`echarts-tooltip.ts`](https://github.com/nqlib/nqchart/blob/main/src/registry/echarts-core/echarts-tooltip.ts).

## Adding or changing a chart

1. Pick the mapping by geometry: shared category axis → `cartesianColumnFocus` /
   `cartesianLineFocus`; per-point item charts (pie, scatter) → `itemFocus`; polar rings → `radialBarSeriesFocus`.
2. Do **not** add `scale`, `shadowBlur` (unless it's a `*-glow` variant), or any
   other hover transform. If a chart "needs" something different, the contract
   changes here first — in this doc and `emphasis-presets.ts` — for **all** charts,
   not just one.
